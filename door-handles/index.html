Door handles, an unnecessarily bad design

Have you pushed a door that should be pulled?

Some doors seem like they should be pushed, when they actually should be pulled. Some look like they should be pulled, when they should be pushed.

We know this. We stick labels on the doors that tell us what to do. And I still get it wrong.

By the way, these doors are called Norman doors after Don Norman who discusses them in his book The Design of Everyday Things.

How to fix it

Have a flat plate for pushing, and a curved handle with a flat surface on the inside for pulling.

Fortunately, when lives are at stake, this is done right; emergency doors often have a horizontal bar that you push. Less stylish, but saves lives.
